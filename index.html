Driver behaviour

Whilst collecting or delivering to TATA Steel sites we expect Drivers to behave in a professional & courteous manner.

The driver should also familiarise themselves with the following local site rules & procedures including:

  • Loading Restraint Guidelines
  • Technical Information sheets
  • Technical Advice

If the driver has any doubts about site rules, he must in the first instance ring his DFDS Logistics Planner for advice or email tata@dfds.com

HANDBRAKE ALARM

Vehicles rolling away are unfortunately an all to common event in the haulage industry. Some events resolve in no injuries, while others end with tragic and fatal consequences. The common issue is the handbrake not applied. One of the best ways to manage, and prevent these events, is to install a handbrake alarm, which effectively prevents the driver from leaving his cab without applying the handbrake.

Some of the minimum features:

  • Operate when engine is on and off
  • Sound if the handbrake is OFF and the driver door is opened
  • Loud enough to be heard clearly and above other noises and alarms (90dB is recommended)
  • Be audible outside the vehicle
  • Only stop when the handbrake is re-applied

If the driver has any doubts about site rules, he must in the first instance ring his DFDS Logistics Planner for advice or email tata@dfds.com
